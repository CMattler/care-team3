Notice of Privacy Practices

Because the Care Team understands that information about their clients is personal, they are committed to maintaining its privacy.

As per guidelines stipulated by the law of the land of India, the team is also required to provide their clients with a Notice of Privacy Practices. This notice is meant to tell the devotee-clients about the team’s legal duties and practices with respect to their (the devotees) personal records and the team is required to abide by the terms of it as are currently in effect.

It describes how medical information about devotee-clients may be used and disclosed and how concerned devotees can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

Ways in which the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team may use and disclose its clients’ personal health records

The following categories describe different ways the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team may use and/or share your personal records without a written authorization from you.

For each category, an explanation is provided, in some cases with examples. These examples are not meant to include all possible types of use and/or disclosure. However, all of the ways the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team is permitted to use and disclose its clients’ personal health records will fall into one of these classifications.

Health Care Operations.

The Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team may use and disclose personal health records for its own operations. These uses and disclosures are necessary for its services and to make sure that all of its clients receive quality care.

Example:

  • The team may use your personal records for quality assessment and performance improvement activities, performance evaluations of its employees as well as future operations planning and development.

Law Enforcement.

The Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team will disclose your personal health records when it is obligatory to do so by any federal, state or local law. As permitted or required by State law, the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team may disclose your personal health records to a law enforcement official for law enforcement/emergency purposes, which include, but are not limited to:

  • As required by law for reporting of certain types of wounds or other physical injuries pursuant to the court order, warrant, subpoena or summons or similar process.
  • Identifying or locating a suspect, fugitive, material witness or missing person.
  • Under certain limited circumstances, when you are the victim of a crime.
  • To a law enforcement official if the team has a suspicion that your death was the result of criminal conduct, including criminal conduct at its facility.
  • In an emergency in order to report a crime.

Public Health Activities.

The Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team may disclose your personal health records for public activities and purposes in order to:

  • Prevent or control disease, injury or disability.
  • Report births or deaths.
  • Report the abuse or neglect of a child or dependent adult.
  • Report reactions to medications or problems with products.
  • Notify individuals exposed to a disease who may be at risk for contracting or spreading the disease.

Abuse, Neglect or Domestic Violence.

  • The Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team is allowed to notify government authorities if they believe a client is the victim of abuse, neglect or domestic violence.
  • The team will make this disclosure only when specifically required or authorized by law or when the client agrees to the disclosure.

Health Oversight Activities.

We may disclose your personal health records to a health oversight agency for activities including audits, civil administrative or criminal investigations, inspections, licensure or disciplinary action.

Judicial and Administrative Proceedings.

We may disclose your personal health records in the course of any judicial or administrative proceeding in response to an order of a court or administrative tribunal as expressly authorized by such order or in response to a subpoena, discovery request or other lawful process, but only when we make reasonable efforts to either notify you about the request or to obtain an order protecting your personal health records.

Coroners, Medical Examiners, Funeral Directors.

We may disclose your personal health records to coroners and medical examiners for purposes of determining your cause of death or for other duties, as authorized by law. We may also disclose your personal health records to funeral directors if necessary to carry out their duties.

Organ, Eye or Tissue Donation.

We may use or disclose your personal health records to organ procurement organizations or other entities engaged in the procurement, banking or transplantation of organs, eyes or tissue for the purpose of facilitating the donation and transplantation.

Serious Threat to Health or Safety.

We may, consistent with applicable law and ethical standards of conduct, disclose your personal health records if we, in good faith, believe that such disclosure is necessary to prevent or lessen a serious and imminent threat to your health or safety or to the health and safety of the public.

Authorization

Uses and/or disclosures of personal health records, other than those described above, will be made only with your written authorization. You may cancel the authorization, at any time, prior to usage and/or disclosures of your personal health records in writing, except to the extent that we have taken an action in reliance on the use or disclosure indicated in the authorization.

Your Rights Regarding Your personal health records

You have the following rights regarding your personal health records.

  1. Right to Request Restrictions/Disclosures – You have the right to request restrictions on certain uses and disclosures of your personal health records. However, we are not required to agree to a requested restriction. If you wish to make a request for restrictions, please submit your written request to the Administrator at the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team Office at Sri Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team, Room 108, Cakra building, ISKCON Mayapur, Sridham Mayapur, Nadia, WB, 741313. (Email correspondence will not be deemed sufficient).

    The following information will NOT be disclosed without your authorization:

    • Psychotherapy notes and/or other types of personal health records related to such therapy.
    • Use of your personal health records for the department’s marketing purposes, including subsidized treatment communications.
    • Any disclosures that constitute a sale of your personal health records.
    • Any other uses or disclosures not already described in this Notice.
  2. Right to Receive Confidential Communications – You have the right to request that communications of your personal health records are done by alternative means or at alternative locations. If you wish to receive confidential communications, please submit your written request to the Administrator at the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team Office at Sri Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team, Room 108, Cakra building, ISKCON Mayapur, Sridham Mayapur, Nadia, WB, 741313.
  3. Right to Inspect and Obtain a Copy of Your personal health records – You have the right to inspect and obtain a copy of your personal health records, as provided by law. If you wish to inspect and obtain a copy of your personal health records, please submit your written request to the Administrator at the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team Office at Sri Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team, Room 108, Cakra building, ISKCON Mayapur, Sridham Mayapur, Nadia, WB, 741313. (Email correspondence will not be deemed sufficient). We may charge you a fee for the cost of copying, mailing or other supplies associated with completing your request.

  4. Right to Amend – You have the right to amend your personal health records, as provided by law. To request an amendment, you must submit a written request including a reason that supports your request to Administrator at the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team Office at Sri Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team, Room 108, Cakra building, ISKCON Mayapur, Sridham Mayapur, Nadia, WB, 741313.

    We may deny your request to amend if:

    • It is not in writing;
    • You do not include a reason for the amendment;
    • The information you wish to amend was not created by us (unless the individual or entity that created the information is no longer available);
    • The information is not part of your personal health records maintained by us;
    • The information is not part of the personal health records you would be permitted to inspect and copy; and/or
    • The information is accurate and complete.

    If you disagree with our denial, you have the right to submit a written statement of disagreement to us. We may prepare a response to your statement and will provide you with a copy.

  5. Right to Receive an Accounting – You have the right to receive an accounting of who your personal health records have been shared with, what was shared and when it was shared. This right applies to those purposes other than treatment, payment or health care operations, as previously described.
  6. Right to Receive a Paper Copy of this Notice – You have the right to receive a paper copy of this Notice at any time. To obtain a copy, please request it from the Administrator at the Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team Office in person.
  7. Right to Receive Notification of Breach of unsecured personal health records – You have the right (and we are required to notify you) to be notified in the event that there is any breach of your personal health records by unauthorized persons/entities.

Changes to this Notice

We reserve the right to change the terms of this notice and to make the revised notice provisions effective for all health information we already have about you, as well as any information we may receive in the future. We will post a copy of the current Notice in a clear and prominent location on our website at: www.mayapurvaishnavacare.com.

A copy of the Notice is also available to you upon request. If we revise the Notice, we will inform you that revisions to the Notice have been made and will inform you of how you can review the revised Notice or request a copy of the revised Notice which will then be sent to you.

Questions Regarding this Notice

If you have any questions or need further information regarding this Notice, you may contact the Mayapur Care Team Administrator on (+91) 947 503 2029.

Complaints

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with us or with the local ISKCON Managerial Authority.

To file a complaint with us, contact the Mayapur Care Team Administrator on (+91) 947 503 2029 or via email at compliance@mayapurvaishnavacare.com

All complaints will be documented in writing. You will not be retaliated against in any way for filing a complaint.
