Testimonials

HH Jayapataka Swami

"One of the threads that keeps us all together is devotee care. The moment that is there, the devotees will naturally like to stay. But the moment it is neglected, they will leave. In the world outside, this culture of care is virtually non-existent. People smile and make you feel that you are going to get some affection but all they are meditating upon is how to exploit you. Only devotees are actually selfless. Only devotees can give love simply to please Krishna. Simply because they see each other as very dear to Krishna."

HH Candramauli Swami

"Devotee care means Vaishnava-seva and Vaishnava-seva is an activity whose value should never be underestimated. The Lord Himself confirmed that it has the potency to awaken within one love for Him and is so important that He would actively seek out opportunities to engage Himself and others in rendering it. Seeing it being made a priority by the Mayapur management and the Vaishnava Care Team is very encouraging and I am sure that it will inspire more people to become devotees as well as keep those devotees who have already joined our society inspired to remain on the path."

Subhangi Devi Dasi

A much loved elderly member of the Mayapur community who requires a great deal of support and help moving around. She has been under the M.V.C.T.’s care since 2014.

Caitanya Nitai Prana Dasa

Caitanya Nitai Prana Dasa has been handicapped for many years. Although he has not allowed his impaired mobility condition to get in the way of his services.

Mohana Devi Dasi

Mohana devi dasi was referred to the M.V.C.T.’s care in 2016. Her old-age forces her to rely on a carer to accompany to most places and needs occasional assistance with her day-to-day activities.

Radha Sundari Devi Dasi

"The Mayapur Vaishnava Care Team plays a very important role in our community by fulfilling one of the most basic needs and desires of mankind - compassionate love. Through their loving and caring approach and service, they are enabling the devotees to experience, in a practical and tangible form, caring, nurturing and healing love, not only for the body but also for the soul. It is a core belief of mine that, as devotees, we are here in community together to practice, in different types of relationships, how to give and receive love, so that eventually we can learn to relish and express real love in our relationship with Krishna. And the bottom line of the Care Team is that it offers, quite simply, LOVE - selflessly extended in a myriad of different ways to all the members of our Vaishnava community. I wish them all the best of luck and hope that they go from strength to strength in continuing to provide this most needed and appreciated service."
